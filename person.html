Biography

Dr Puey Ungphakorn (1916–1999) served as Governor of the Bank of Thailand from 1959 to 1971, a tenure marked by economic reform, modernisation of the Thai financial system, and the establishment of the Thammasat University Faculty of Economics.

Born in Bangkok, Puey received his doctorate in economics from the London School of Economics. He is widely considered the most influential central banker in modern Thai history, credited with steering the economy through a period of sustained growth and institutionalising the independence of the Bank of Thailand.

His signature appears on banknotes from the Ninth through Eleventh Series, spanning denominations from ฿ 1 to ฿ 1000. Notes bearing his signature alongside Finance Ministers Somsak Kittikhachorn, Sompop Bhalakul, and Bunchana Atthakor are among the most historically significant of the mid-century era.

After leaving the Bank, Dr Puey became a vocal advocate for democratic reform. He died in London in 1999 and is memorialised on the Thammasat University campus.
