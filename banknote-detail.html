Description

The One Hundred Baht note of the Fourteenth Series was issued on 18 November 2004. It represents one of the most widely circulated denominations of its era, characterised by a distinctive red-brown palette and enhanced security features introduced following the 1997 financial crisis.

The obverse features a formal portrait of King Bhumibol Adulyadej in royal regalia, flanked by the Royal Garuda emblem. The reverse depicts Wat Phra Kaew within the Grand Palace compound.

Printed by Thomas De La Rue in England, incorporating an electrotype watermark, a metallic security thread, and micro-printing along the borders.
